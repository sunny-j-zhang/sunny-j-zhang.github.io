# June 27 (Component Selection)

An iteration of the cycloidal gearbox was printed to verify the fit and geometries of those specified in the CAD. Although there were some minor tolerancing issues mostly as a result of 3D printing, it appears to be working as expected and the design will be further fine tuned for the specific hardware to be purchased for fitting and testing with bearings. This will go along with a more complete CAD of the structural components of the arm to ensure the gearbox geometries mesh well with the remainder of the design. This will be completed in the coming weeks in the month of July. In parallel, the basic hardware has been acquired to set up flashing and testing of an image on the RPi4, which will also be explored. Another goal for July is to investigate further into the onboard microcontroller for motor control and how it interfaces with the RPi4. A power distribution plan will also need to be in place to ensure the motors require sufficient power and the RPi4 is also sufficiently protected from excessive voltage and current. This will likely require a power distribution board, which will also facilitate the interface between the RPi4 and the microcontroller. The shoulder (Nema 23) and the elbow (Nema 17) motors will require 24V power whereas the wrist/end effector (FIT1034) motors will require 12V power.
